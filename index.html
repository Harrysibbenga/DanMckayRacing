“ ...for 2019 McLaren has invited Daniel to be one of the two hand-picked drivers to step up to the European GT4 Championship to race the lead 570S GT4 car in a McLaren-supported programme aimed to win the championship ”

Dan McKay

Proving his racing abilities as he has progressed through the ranks in the seven years he’s been in competition, 27-year old Dan McKay is poised to make his name on the European stage in 2019.

After a successful few years in some of the UK’s most competitive national series, including four years racing Legends, he took class victories in Porsche Carrera Cup GB and the British GT Championship, Dan will be showcasing his talents in the GT4 European Series this season.

Building on a successful year in which he was named British GT’s ‘Sunoco Fastest GT4 Driver of the Year’ and the fastest GT4 Silver class driver, according to statisticians The B Pillar, Edinburgh-born Dan will be testing himself against the continent’s best over 12 tough events.

The Championship

Organised by the successful SRO Motorsports Group, the GT4 European Series benefits from the same principles that have made high-profile championships like the Blancpain GT Series so successful, namely reduced costs, technical fairness, dynamic races featuring professional and amateur racers, convenient locations, and a friendly paddock atmosphere.

With all of the series’ races sharing the bill with the Blancpain World Challenge Europe or Blancpain GT Series Endurance Cup, it ensures there is strong media coverage throughout the season. It is also the only international SRO-organised GT4 series to accept Silver-Silver graded driver pairings, making the GT4 European Series a perfect stepping stone towards GT3 for young and talented competitors.

Mclaren

Launched in 2010, and the largest part of the McLaren Group, McLaren Automotive creates high- performance, luxury sportscars and supercars, all hand-assembled in Woking at the McLaren Production Centre.

A pioneering brand which has consistently pushed the boundaries, McLaren will invest £1bn in research and development to deliver 15 new cars or derivatives by the end of 2022, of which at least half will be hybrids. Indeed, it was the first to deliver a hybrid hypercar: the stunning McLaren P1.

McLaren Automotive has three distinct product families: Sports Series, Super Series and Ultimate Series, which are available through over 80 retailers in 30 markets around the world. It partners with world-leading companies to provide specialist expertise and technology in support of its development, engineering, and manufacture.
