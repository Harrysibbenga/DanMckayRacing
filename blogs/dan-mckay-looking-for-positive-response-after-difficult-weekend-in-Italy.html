Dan McKay looking for positive response after difficult weekend in Italy

Scottish racer Dan McKay had a challenging trip to Misano in the GT4 European Series last weekend (June 28-30) as he continues battles for the championship title.

High ambient temperatures combined with Balance of Performance [BoP] changes to his Equipe Vershcuur McLaren 570S GT4 meant Dan and team-mate Benjamin Lessennes struggled with handling each time they went on to track.

Losing the performance of his Pirelli tyres after one lap meant Dan suffered an uncharacteristic qualifying session, leaving him with plenty to do from a lower grid position than he’d hoped for.

With Ben taking the first stint in race one, Dan climbed aboard at the half-hour mark but found it hard to make progress as he started to suffer with failing brakes. Frustratingly he was passed by rival cars on track before he had to bring the car in to retire when they failed completely.

The Scot started race two and after falling back a few places early on did well to settle in to a rhythm and climb back up five places, handing over to Ben from 14th position, though a full course yellow just as the pair finished their pit stop cost places on track. However, Ben did well to manage the car and begin to gain positions, clawing his way back up to 11th overall and ninth in the Silver Cup class.

Despite falling to third in the championship standings, Dan and Ben are keen to return to action as the series travels to Zandvoort in the Netherlands on July 12-14. Coverage of qualifying and both one-hour races will be carried live via the championship website, with links shared via Dan’s official social media channels.

Dan Mckay

“Our BoP changed before the weekend and we got 30kg added and the weight made no sense to us. Ben did a good job in qualifying to end up where he did, but my session did not go as well. It seemed like the tyres had gone after one lap so I need to work on my understanding of the car and tyres when the track is hot to be better at that."

"We were going to finish well in race one. Ben pitted in the podium places but as soon as I came out the brakes began to fail which made a bad situation worse, so I went backwards and then the pedal went to the floor and that was it.”

“The team were brilliant, the car was set up well, but a combination of bad factors made it tough to drive. The brakes failed in race one not because of the team or anything to do with McLaren, it was just a series of unfortunate events."

“We tried to find a better balance for race two. I wasn’t too happy with my stint though I handed the car over in an okay position and didn’t use the tyres so Ben could push on. Unfortunately, the full course yellow meant we lost 30-60 seconds which cost us a lot of points."

“We’ve lost the championship lead, but we see Zandvoort and the Nürburgring as strong circuits for us and we think we can end the year with good results, so it is all to play for. We have to do the best we can and I’m sure we’ll come back stronger, though it’s a shame not to continue our streak of podium finishes.”
