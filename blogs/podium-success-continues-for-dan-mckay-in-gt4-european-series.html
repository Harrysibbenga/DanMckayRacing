Podium success continues for Dan McKay in GT4 European Series.

Scottish racer Dan McKay secured a fourth podium finish of the season in the competitive GT4 European Series as he and team-mate Benjamin Lessennes finished second in race two at Paul Ricard last weekend (May 31-June 2) to remain at the top of the championship standings.

After disappointment in race one when contact ended Dan’s race in the opening laps, and a subsequent five-place grid penalty for race two, the Equipe Verschuur duo of Ben and Dan fought back for a superb second-place finish in their McLaren 570S GT4.

Being baulked on a quick lap in qualifying, Dan started a lowly 11th for the first contest. After a messy first lap where rival drivers were all over the track, the McLaren driver fell to 15th but was quickly up five places to tenth when he had to retire with damage.

His colleague Ben did a sterling job in the team’s rapid McLaren 570S GT4 during Sunday’s race as he climbed from ninth up to second during his stint, passing cars all around the French Grand Prix venue, which included a good battle with Dan’s brother Euan.

After the mandatory pit stop, Dan resumed in third, which became second as the car ahead took a penalty for a short pit stop. From there on it was simply a case of bringing the car home without risking anything for further silverware and redemption after the previous day’s struggle.

Dan, Ben, and Equipe Verschuur will next be in action at Misano World Circuit Marco Simoncelli in Italy at the end of June to begin the second half of the GT4 European Series season, where they will look to increase their championship lead.

Dan Mckay

“It was a weekend of mixed fortunes for us. Things were looking great in both practice sessions, but as qualifying started a roll bar failed, which left the car hobbled for the session. Some traffic on a quick lap cost me around 0.3s and when the grid is so close that meant starting down in 11th which isn’t where we want to be."

"Starting down there meant overdriving which led to a mistake. I was coming up on the #62 Aston Martin which had gone to the outside, so I braked early for the corner – 20kph slower than any lap I did across the weekend – and he turned in on me. I don’t believe it was malicious, I just don’t think he knew I was there. Neither team protested, but the stewards handed us a penalty despite the DNF, demoting Ben to ninth for race two. He did a great job, got up to second place, and gave me a great car to finish the race with.”

“Equipe Verschuur were fantastic all weekend. They were awake until 3 am on Sunday morning fixing the damage from race one, so I can only thank them for how much effort they put in to help us get the podium."

“Next up for us is Misano and the car has a good history there. It’s been great to be on the podium so much this season, but we need to eradicate the small mistakes if we want to keep on course for a championship title.”
